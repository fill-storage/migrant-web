This guide provides an overview of your legal rights as a migrant worker in Qatar. It is essential to understand these rights to protect yourself and ensure fair treatment.

<SectionContainer title="Introduction to Qatari Labor Law" slug="introduction">
  Qatari Labor Law provides a framework for employment relationships in Qatar.
  It outlines the rights and responsibilities of both employers and employees.
  Key aspects of the law include provisions for contracts, working hours, wages,
  and termination.
</SectionContainer>

![My Legal Contract](/legal-contract.png)

<SectionContainer title="Employment Contract Rights" slug="contract-rights">
  Your employment contract is a legally binding agreement that specifies the
  terms and conditions of your employment. You have the right to:
  <ul>
    <li>Receive a written contract in a language you understand.</li>
    <li>
      Have the contract specify your job title, responsibilities, and salary.
    </li>
    <li>Ensure the contract complies with Qatari Labor Law.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer title="Working Hours and Overtime" slug="working-hours">
  Qatari Labor Law regulates working hours to protect employees from overwork.
  You have the right to:
  <ul>
    <li>
      A maximum of 48 working hours per week (8 hours per day, 6 days per week).
    </li>
    <li>
      Overtime pay for any hours worked beyond the standard 48 hours per week.
    </li>
    <li>A weekly rest day, typically on Friday.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer title="Wages and Payment" slug="wages-payment">
  You have the right to receive your wages on time and in full, as specified in
  your employment contract. Key rights include:
  <ul>
    <li>A minimum wage (if applicable).</li>
    <li>Payment of wages at regular intervals (e.g., monthly).</li>
    <li>Protection against unlawful deductions from your wages.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer title="Accommodation Rights" slug="accommodation-rights">
  If your employer provides accommodation, it must meet certain standards of
  safety and hygiene. You have the right to:
  <ul>
    <li>Safe and sanitary living conditions.</li>
    <li>Adequate space and privacy.</li>
    <li>Access to essential amenities.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer title="Healthcare Rights" slug="healthcare-rights">
  Your employer is legally required to provide you with health insurance that
  covers basic medical services. You have the right to:
  <ul>
    <li>Access to healthcare services when you need them.</li>
    <li>
      Coverage for doctor visits, hospital stays, and emergency treatment.
    </li>
    <li>Information about your health insurance policy.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer title="Right to End Employment" slug="termination-rights">
  Both you and your employer have the right to terminate the employment
  contract, subject to certain conditions. You have the right to:
  <ul>
    <li>Resign from your job, provided you give the required notice period.</li>
    <li>Receive end-of-service benefits (if eligible) upon termination.</li>
    <li>Protection against unfair dismissal.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer title="Grievance Procedures" slug="grievance-procedures">
  If you have a complaint or grievance related to your employment, you have the
  right to:
  <ul>
    <li>Raise your concerns with your employer.</li>
    <li>Seek assistance from the Ministry of Labour.</li>
    <li>File a formal complaint if necessary.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer
  title="Protection from Discrimination"
  slug="discrimination-protection"
>
  Qatari Labor Law prohibits discrimination based on race, religion, gender, or
  nationality. You have the right to:
  <ul>
    <li>Equal treatment in the workplace.</li>
    <li>Protection against harassment and discrimination.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>

<SectionContainer title="Access to Justice" slug="access-to-justice">
  If your rights are violated, you have the right to seek legal redress through
  the Qatari courts. This includes the right to:
  <ul>
    <li>File a lawsuit against your employer.</li>
    <li>Obtain legal representation.</li>
    <li>Seek compensation for damages.</li>
  </ul>
</SectionContainer>
